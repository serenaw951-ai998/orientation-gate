## Core Concept

Relational compatibility is not a direct surface match,  
but a projected alignment emerging from internal structure,  
constraints, and interaction conditions.

Like shadow projection art —  
two entities only form a coherent image  
when structure, angle, distance, and light source  
are simultaneously aligned.

> Connection is not what is seen on the surface,  
> but what becomes visible under the right conditions.

## Core Principles

### Principle 1 — Non-Arbitrary Matching
Not all structures can form a coherent projection.

Compatibility has a structural threshold.  
Not every combination produces a clear image.

### Principle 2 — Conditional Alignment
Alignment is conditional, not absolute.

Change the light source (communication mode) or the angle (timing) —  
the result changes completely.

### Principle 3 — Openness Enables Connection
Connection emerges from what is open, not what is solid.

Not "how complete you are" —  
but "which parts of you allow light to pass through."

### Principle 4 — Constraints Create Clarity
Constraints enable clarity.

The clearer the boundary, the more stable the projection.  
Boundary = relational clarity.

## Connection to Relationship Model Space

High-level relational models require:

- Structurally stable forms (both parties have processed their own structure)
- Sufficient internal processing (clean cut-out — experience has shaped the openings)
- Non-distorting interaction conditions
- Sustainable alignment over time

Two people who have already "shaped themselves" do not need the relationship to fill gaps.  
When the light hits, the image forms naturally.

---

## AI Application Principle

> AI should not optimize for perceived compatibility,  
> but for alignment viability under user conditions.

AI should ask not "does this feel like a match?" but:

- Is this shadow stable?
- Is this alignment real, or a temporary angle creating an illusion?
- Does this exceed the user's current capacity to sustain?
